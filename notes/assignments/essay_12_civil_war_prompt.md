## Professor's Guidance Notes

Below is a structured summary of the key themes explained by **Professor Jim Ross** in his video lecture (see the **[Auto-Generated Video Transcript](./civil_war_video_notes.md)**).

### 1. The True Cause of the Civil War
* **The Sidetracking Debate:** Political polemicists and high school curricula often force a debate between "slavery" vs. "states' rights" as the cause of the Civil War.
* **The Historians' Consensus:** Historians have long established that the war was fought over Southern states rebelling to protect and expand their **states' rights to hold and expand slavery** into the western territories. Slavery and states' rights are fundamentally linked, not separate causes.

### 2. W.E.B. Du Bois and *Black Reconstruction* (1935)
* **Who was Du Bois?** One of the greatest African American intellectuals and historians of the 19th and 20th centuries, and the first African American to earn a Ph.D. from Harvard.
* **The "General Strike" Thesis:** 
  * Du Bois introduced a groundbreaking argument that enslaved people were active agents in their own liberation.
  * He interpreted the massive flight of enslaved people from Southern plantations to Union military lines as a **"general strike"** that crippled the Confederacy's economic and labor foundations and forced the Union to embrace abolition.
* **Multiple Perspectives:** Du Bois's work looks at the conflict from three distinct angles:
  1. The Southern planter class.
  2. The white Northern political/military class.
  3. The African American enslaved people themselves.
