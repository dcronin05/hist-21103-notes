## Professor's Guidance Notes

Below is a structured summary of the key themes explained by **Professor Jim Ross** in the **[Tumult and Liberty Video Lecture](../media/Horn,%20Chapter%205.mov)** (click for full **[Auto-Generated Video Transcript](./tumult_and_liberty_video_notes.md)**).

### 1. The Surprising Crisis of 1622
* **A Coordinated Blow:** In March 1622, the Jamestown colony was finally starting to grow and become profitable. However, on March 22, 1622, the Powhatan Confederacy (a powerful network of Native groups led by Powhatan's successor, Opechancanough) launched a massive, coordinated surprise attack on plantations up and down the James River.
* **The Damage:** Kills approximately 350 English colonists (men, women, and children) in brutal, hand-to-hand fighting, throwing the colony into total crisis.

### 2. Social Construction & Dehumanization of Native Americans
* **Pre-1622 Paternalism:** Before the attack, the English had focused on converting Native Americans to Christianity and integrating them into a shared, inclusive society.
* **The Shift to Dehumanization:** Following the massacre, colonists socially constructed a radically different image of the Native American:
  * Cast them as "inhuman", "spawn of Satan", "irrational", and "not created in the image of God."
  * Christopher Brooke's contemporary poem is a prime example: *"natives rooted in evil, opposed to good, errors of nature, of inhuman birth... the very dread, garbage and spawn of Earth."*
  * **Consequence:** This construct justified stripping Native Americans of their land and systematically expelling them from the colony—setting a precedent that would define future American policy.

### 3. Empowerment of Sandys's Factional Enemies
* **The Failure of the Commonwealth:** In London, Sandys's political rivals used the tragedy to argue that his "popular" (liberty-focused) reforms and ideas of a Commercial/Christian Commonwealth had failed, leading directly to disaster and collapse.
* **Royal Encroachment:** King James I, who detested Parliament and wanted to return England to an absolute monarchy, used the massacre as an excuse to crack down on the Virginia Company.

### 4. Post-1622 Governance: Mixed vs. Absolute Rule
* **commissioners Warning:** Royal officials sent to Virginia argued that the colony was crippled by too many "popular voices" and warned that *"extreme liberty was worse than extreme tyranny."* They attempted to curtail the power of the House of Burgesses.
* **Emergence of Mixed Government:** In response, Virginia colonists began championing a "mixed government" / "republican" model (Monarchy at the top, local elites acting as Lords, and representation for the common freeholders in the assembly) as a safeguard against royal tyranny, keeping representative government alive despite the company's dissolution.
