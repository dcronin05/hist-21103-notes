## Professor's Guidance Notes

Below is a structured summary of the key themes explained by **Professor Jim Ross** in his video lecture (see the **[Auto-Generated Video Transcript](./columbus_essay_video_notes.md)**).

### 1. The Traditional Narrative (The Myth)

- **The Claim:** Columbus is portrayed as a modern Renaissance explorer who proved the Earth was round while others believed it was flat, fighting against religious superstition.
- **The Goal:** Simply seeking gold, personal wealth, and expanding European domain.

### 2. The Historical & Scientific Reality

- **The Round Earth:** Educated people in Europe and North Africa already knew the Earth was round (proven by the ancient Greeks thousands of years prior).
- **The Error:** Columbus's proposal was rejected by other nations because his science was faulty. Based on his reading of the biblical Books of Revelation and Daniel, he mistakenly calculated that the Earth was much smaller than it actually is.
- **The Survival Factor:** If the Americas had not been in the way, Columbus and his crew would have starved, as his ships were not structured for a voyage of that scale.

### 3. The Hamdani Thesis (The Crusade)

- **Devout Mystic:** Abbas Hamdani argues that Columbus was a deeply religious, mystical Christian influenced by 15th-century apocalyptic beliefs.
- **The Mongol Alliance:** Columbus believed in a historical legend that a group of Christians existed in China (the Grand Khan / Mongols who had supposedly converted). He sailed west intending to contact them.
- **Recapturing Jerusalem:** His ultimate, hidden motivation was to unite with the Mongol forces, form a grand army, and march west to reclaim **Jerusalem** from Muslim control, fulfilling what he believed were end-times prophecies.

### 4. The Core Essay Objective

- **Compare & Contrast:** Contrast the self-interest/scientific myth (traditional biography) with the crusading/apocalyptic thesis (Hamdani).
- **Evaluate the Evidence:** Analyze the types of evidence each narrative relies on (e.g., public records and logs vs. private apocalyptic letters and marginalia).
- **Reflect on Historiography:** Consider how historical interpretations change when new evidence is uncovered, and why the stories we choose to believe matter.
